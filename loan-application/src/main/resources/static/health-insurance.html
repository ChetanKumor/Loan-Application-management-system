Health Insurance

Overview

Health insurance is a type of insurance that covers medical expenses incurred due to illness, injury, or other health conditions. It provides financial protection against high medical costs and ensures access to quality healthcare.

Key Features:

  • Comprehensive coverage for hospitalization expenses
  • Coverage for pre and post hospitalization expenses
  • Day care procedures coverage
  • Cashless treatment at network hospitals
  • No medical check-up required for individuals below 45 years
  • Tax benefits under Section 80D of Income Tax Act

Benefits:

  • Protection against rising healthcare costs
  • Coverage for family members under a single policy
  • Access to quality healthcare without financial burden
  • Cumulative bonus for claim-free years
  • Free health check-ups after specified period

Required Documents

Identity Proof (Any one):

  • Passport
  • Voter ID Card
  • Driving License
  • Aadhaar Card
  • PAN Card

Address Proof (Any one):

  • Passport
  • Electricity Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Telephone Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Water Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Aadhaar Card

Age Proof:

  • Birth Certificate
  • Passport
  • Driving License
  • School/College Certificate

Medical Documents (if applicable):

  • Previous medical records
  • Medical examination reports
  • Existing health insurance policy details

Policy Eligibility

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Age: Entry age from 3 months to 65 years
  • Family Coverage: Self, spouse, dependent children, and parents
  • Medical Check-up: Required for individuals above 45 years or with pre-existing conditions

Coverage Amount:

The sum insured ranges from ₹1 lakh to ₹1 crore, depending on your requirements and premium payment capacity.

Policy Term:

Health insurance policies are typically issued for 1 year and can be renewed annually. Some insurers also offer multi-year policies with discounts.

! Important Note !

  • Pre-existing diseases are covered after a waiting period of 2-4 years, depending on the policy.
  • Certain procedures and treatments may have sub-limits or co-payment clauses.
  • Maternity benefits are available after a waiting period of 2-4 years in specific plans.
  • Premium amount may increase with age at the time of renewal.
  • Non-disclosure of medical conditions may lead to claim rejection.
  • Some policies have room rent limits for hospitalization.
  • Certain diseases and treatments may be permanently excluded from coverage.

Helpful Tips

Before Buying:

  • Assess your healthcare needs and family medical history
  • Compare coverage, exclusions, and waiting periods across policies
  • Check the network hospitals in your vicinity
  • Understand sub-limits and co-payment clauses
  • Review claim settlement ratio of the insurance company

During Application:

  • Disclose all pre-existing conditions honestly
  • Read the policy document carefully
  • Understand the claim process
  • Clarify doubts about coverage and exclusions

After Purchase:

  • Keep policy documents safe and accessible
  • Note the policy renewal date
  • Inform family members about the policy details
  • Keep emergency contact numbers of the insurer handy
  • Review your coverage annually and upgrade if needed
