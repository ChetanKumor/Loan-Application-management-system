Life Insurance

Overview

Life insurance is a contract between an insurer and a policyholder that provides financial protection to the family in case of the policyholder's death. It ensures that your loved ones are financially secure even in your absence.

Key Features:

  • Death benefit to nominees/beneficiaries
  • Various policy options including Term, Whole Life, Endowment, and ULIPs
  • Riders for additional coverage like critical illness, accidental death, etc.
  • Loan facility against policy
  • Tax benefits under Section 80C and 10(10D) of Income Tax Act
  • Premium payment options: Monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or annually

Benefits:

  • Financial security for dependents
  • Debt repayment in case of untimely death
  • Wealth creation through investment-linked policies
  • Tax-efficient investment option
  • Peace of mind knowing your family is protected
  • Retirement planning through pension plans

Required Documents

Identity Proof (Any one):

  • Passport
  • Voter ID Card
  • Driving License
  • Aadhaar Card
  • PAN Card

Address Proof (Any one):

  • Passport
  • Electricity Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Telephone Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Water Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Aadhaar Card

Age Proof:

  • Birth Certificate
  • Passport
  • Driving License
  • School/College Certificate

Income Proof:

  • Salary slips for the last 3 months
  • Income Tax Returns for the last 2 years
  • Bank statements for the last 6 months
  • Form 16

Other Documents:

  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Medical reports (if required)
  • Nominee details with proof of identity

Policy Eligibility

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Age: Minimum entry age is 18 years, maximum varies by policy type (typically up to 65 years)
  • Health Status: Medical examination may be required based on age, sum assured, and policy type
  • Income: Minimum income requirements vary based on the sum assured
  • Residency: Indian resident or NRI (conditions apply)

Coverage Amount:

The sum assured depends on your income, age, and financial goals. Generally, it is recommended to have life cover of at least 10-15 times your annual income.

Policy Term:

Term insurance: 5-40 years or up to a certain age (e.g., 75 years)
Whole Life: Coverage until 99-100 years of age
Endowment/ULIP: 10-30 years

! Important Note !

  • Non-disclosure of material facts may lead to claim rejection.
  • Suicide clause: Death due to suicide within 12 months of policy issuance or revival is not covered.
  • Grace period for premium payment is typically 15-30 days.
  • Policy lapses if premium is not paid within the grace period.
  • Revival of lapsed policy is possible within 2-5 years with additional charges.
  • Surrender value is available only for policies other than term insurance.
  • Nomination is essential for smooth claim settlement.
  • Tax benefits are subject to changes in tax laws.

Helpful Tips

Before Buying:

  • Assess your life insurance needs based on financial goals and liabilities
  • Compare different policy types and their features
  • Check the claim settlement ratio of insurance companies
  • Understand the terms and conditions, especially exclusions
  • Consider inflation when deciding the sum assured

During Application:

  • Disclose all medical conditions honestly
  • Fill the proposal form carefully without any errors
  • Choose nominees wisely and inform them about the policy
  • Understand the premium payment schedule
  • Read the policy document thoroughly before signing

After Purchase:

  • Keep policy documents in a safe place and share their location with nominees
  • Pay premiums on time to avoid policy lapse
  • Review your life insurance coverage periodically
  • Update nominee details if there are changes in family circumstances
  • Inform the insurer about any change in address or contact details
