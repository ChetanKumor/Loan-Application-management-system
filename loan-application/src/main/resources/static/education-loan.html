Education Loan

Overview

An education loan is designed to help students pursue higher education in India or abroad. It covers tuition fees, accommodation, books, equipment, and other education-related expenses.

Key Features:

  • Competitive interest rates starting from 7.15% p.a.
  • Loan amount up to ₹75 lakhs for studies abroad and ₹30 lakhs for studies in India
  • Flexible repayment tenure of up to 15 years
  • Moratorium period covering course duration plus 6-12 months
  • No collateral required for loans up to ₹7.5 lakhs
  • 100% financing available for deserving students

Benefits:

  • Tax benefits under Section 80E of Income Tax Act
  • No prepayment or foreclosure charges
  • Covers a wide range of courses and institutions
  • Special concessions for female students

Required Documents

Student Documents:

  • Completed loan application form
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Identity proof (Passport/Voter ID/Aadhaar Card)
  • Address proof (Passport/Aadhaar Card/Utility bills)
  • Academic records (10th, 12th, Graduation certificates)
  • Entrance exam scores (GRE, GMAT, CAT, etc.)
  • Admission letter from the institution
  • Cost of study details from the institution

Co-applicant/Guarantor Documents:

  • Identity proof
  • Address proof
  • Income proof (Salary slips, Form 16, ITR)
  • Bank statements for the last 6 months
  • Employment details

Collateral Documents (for loans above ₹7.5 lakhs):

  • Property documents
  • Fixed deposit receipts
  • Life insurance policies
  • Any other security documents

Loan Eligibility

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Student: Indian citizen with admission to a recognized institution
  • Co-applicant: Parent/Guardian/Spouse with stable income
  • Academic Record: Good academic background with consistent performance
  • Course Eligibility: Professional/Technical courses from recognized institutions

Loan Amount:

The maximum loan amount depends on the course, institution, and location of study. Generally, loans up to ₹75 lakhs are available for studies abroad and up to ₹30 lakhs for studies in India.

Repayment Period:

The repayment period can extend up to 15 years after the moratorium period (course duration + 6-12 months).

! Important Note !

  • The interest rate and loan amount are subject to change based on the applicant's profile, course, and institution.
  • Processing fee may be applicable as per bank policy.
  • Interest subsidy available for economically weaker sections under government schemes.
  • Interest accrues during the moratorium period but payment is deferred.
  • Loan disbursement is usually made directly to the institution.
  • The bank may request additional documents during the verification process.
  • Approval is subject to verification of documents and creditworthiness of co-applicant.

Helpful Tips

Before Applying:

  • Research and compare education loan options from different banks
  • Check the list of institutions covered under the bank's approved list
  • Calculate the total cost of education including living expenses
  • Prepare all documents well in advance
  • Check eligibility for government subsidy schemes

During Application:

  • Apply at least 2-3 months before the fee payment deadline
  • Provide accurate information to avoid rejection
  • Understand the interest calculation method (simple or compound)
  • Clarify all terms and conditions before signing

After Approval:

  • Keep track of disbursements and ensure they match the fee structure
  • Start making interest payments during the study period if possible
  • Maintain good academic performance
  • Keep the bank updated about any changes in contact details or course
