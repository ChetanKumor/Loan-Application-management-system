Vehicle Insurance

Overview

Vehicle insurance provides financial protection against physical damage or bodily injury resulting from traffic accidents and against liability that could arise from incidents in a vehicle. It also covers damage to your vehicle due to theft, fire, natural calamities, etc.

Key Features:

  • Third-party liability coverage (mandatory by law)
  • Comprehensive coverage for own damage
  • Personal accident cover for owner-driver
  • Add-on covers like zero depreciation, engine protection, roadside assistance, etc.
  • No Claim Bonus (NCB) for claim-free years
  • Cashless claims at network garages

Benefits:

  • Legal compliance with Motor Vehicles Act
  • Financial protection against accidents and damages
  • Coverage for third-party liabilities
  • Peace of mind while driving
  • Cashless repairs at network garages
  • Discounts for claim-free years through NCB

Required Documents

For New Insurance:

  • Vehicle Registration Certificate (RC)
  • Invoice of the vehicle (for new vehicles)
  • Previous insurance policy (for policy renewal)
  • Valid driving license
  • Identity proof (Passport/Voter ID/Aadhaar Card/PAN Card)
  • Address proof (Passport/Aadhaar Card/Utility bills)
  • Passport-sized photographs

For Claims Processing:

  • Duly filled claim form
  • Copy of insurance policy
  • Copy of Registration Certificate (RC)
  • Copy of driving license
  • FIR copy (in case of theft, third-party property damage, or bodily injury)
  • Original repair bills and payment receipts (for reimbursement claims)
  • Photographs of the damaged vehicle (if available)

Policy Eligibility

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Vehicle Ownership: The vehicle should be registered in your name
  • Vehicle Age: For new comprehensive policies, the vehicle should typically be less than 10 years old (varies by insurer)
  • Valid Registration: The vehicle must have valid registration with the RTO
  • Vehicle Type: Coverage available for two-wheelers, cars, commercial vehicles, etc.

Coverage Types:

  • Third-Party Insurance: Covers damages to third-party person or property
  • Comprehensive Insurance: Covers both third-party liability and own damage
  • Add-on Covers: Additional protections like zero depreciation, engine protection, etc.

Policy Term:

For new vehicles:
- Two-wheelers: 5 years (third-party) or 1-5 years (comprehensive)
- Four-wheelers: 3 years (third-party) or 1-3 years (comprehensive)
For existing vehicles: Annual renewal or multi-year options

! Important Note !

  • Third-party insurance is mandatory by law for all vehicles plying on Indian roads.
  • Driving without valid insurance can lead to penalties and legal consequences.
  • Claims may be rejected if the vehicle is driven without a valid driving license or under the influence of alcohol/drugs.
  • Modifications to the vehicle should be informed to the insurer, as undeclared modifications may lead to claim rejection.
  • No Claim Bonus (NCB) is transferable when you sell your vehicle and buy a new one.
  • Premium is calculated based on factors like vehicle type, age, cubic capacity, geographical zone, etc.
  • Depreciation is applicable on parts replaced during repairs unless you have zero depreciation cover.
  • There is a time limit for claim intimation, usually 24-48 hours for accidents and 7 days for theft.

Helpful Tips

Before Buying:

  • Compare premiums, coverage, and claim settlement ratio of different insurers
  • Check the list of network garages in your vicinity
  • Understand the Insured Declared Value (IDV) and how it affects your premium
  • Consider useful add-ons like zero depreciation, engine protection, etc.
  • Check for discounts like No Claim Bonus, anti-theft devices, etc.

During Policy Period:

  • Keep your insurance policy document in the vehicle
  • Inform the insurer about any modifications to the vehicle
  • Follow traffic rules to avoid accidents and maintain NCB
  • Know the claim process and emergency contact numbers
  • Renew your policy before it expires to maintain continuous coverage

During Claims:

  • Inform the insurer immediately after an accident
  • Take photographs of the damaged vehicle from different angles
  • File an FIR in case of theft or third-party involvement
  • Don't repair the vehicle before the insurer's inspection
  • Keep all original bills and documents for reimbursement claims
